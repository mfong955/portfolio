About This Work

My PhD and postdoctoral research focused on cosmological simulations and dark matter halo dynamics. This work required processing billions of data points and presenting complex Bayesian statistical analyses in ways that could be understood by researchers across different specializations.

The figures below are from my paper "A natural boundary of dark matter haloes revealed around the minimum bias and maximum infall locations", published in MNRAS. Each figure represents a different approach to visualizing multi-dimensional statistical relationships.

What This Demonstrates

Ability to distill complex multi-parameter analyses into clear visual narratives. These skills translate directly to creating architecture diagrams, workflow visualizations, and data-driven content in technical documentation.

Figure 1: Multi-Parameter Bias Profiles

Visual design choices: Six-panel grid allows direct comparison across parameters. Consistent color gradients indicate parameter progression. Log-scale axes handle the large dynamic range while maintaining readability. The horizontal dotted line provides a visual anchor at unity bias.

Figure 4: Two-Parameter Space Mapping

Visual design choices: This 6×6 matrix visualization enables readers to quickly identify which parameter combinations most strongly influence the measured quantity. The overlay of measured values (thin lines) against model predictions (thick lines) makes it immediately apparent where the model succeeds or fails—no need to compare separate figures.

Figure 8: Theory Illustration

Visual design choices: This conceptual illustration bridges the gap between raw data and physical interpretation. By showing the before/after states with clear directional arrows, readers can immediately grasp how matter flows toward the halo center while the outer regions become depleted—a key insight that would be difficult to convey through data plots alone.

Figure 9: Phase Space Dynamics

Visual design choices: Phase space diagrams are notoriously difficult to interpret. Here, the density colormapping with layered contours allows readers to perceive both the overall structure and quantitative boundaries. The consistent vertical line annotations across panels enable direct comparison of how physical boundaries shift with halo mass.

Technical Methods

  • Tools: Python (matplotlib, seaborn), custom visualization pipelines
  • Data scale: 8+ billion particles from N-body cosmological simulations
  • Statistical methods: MCMC sampling, Gaussian Process Regression, Bayesian parameter estimation with full covariance matrices
